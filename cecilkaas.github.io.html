Career Progress

Read time: 2 mins

This week, we focus on the GMAT. Here is a breakdown of our recommended preparation process

For the past 3 weeks, I have been transitioning away from full time work at a cafe in my home town to refocusing my efforts on acedemia. Currently, I am trying to study for an undergraduate degree in Economics. In my final year, I am realising that there are still things that I need to complete in order to continue my educational journey and one thing that is becoming more paramount for continuing my education is taking the GMAT. This is an exam that some business schools require some students to take before enrollment to guage their skills in their quantitative, comprehension and analystical thinking abilities. At first, I was disheartened at the idea of taking a very difficult maths-based test however, in my hour of dismay, I was reminded of the wise words of everyone's favourite Regional Manager of the best paper company in the world.

"You miss 100% of the shots you don't take" - Michael Scott

Part of my transition into university involved evolving my attitude towards learning about difficult things and since then, I have found myself tackling things that I felt unequpit to face before head on. The GMAT has become my most recent challenge.
